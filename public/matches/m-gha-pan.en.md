## home_note
Ghana make their fifth World Cup appearance with a squad well-represented across Europe's top five leagues. Queiroz deploys a 4-3-3, with the attack led by Iñaki Williams (Athletic Club), Antoine Semenyo (Manchester City), and Jordan Ayew — a combination of pace and technical quality. Thomas Partey (Villarreal) anchors the midfield as Ghana's engine. Key star Mohammed Kudus is absent through injury, significantly reducing Ghana's creative output. Ghana qualified steadily through Africa but will need to find their rhythm in this major tournament opener.

## away_note
Panama are on their second World Cup adventure — their first (2018) ended with three defeats and no goals scored. Coach Christiansen has built a disciplined 3-4-2-1 based on organised defending and counter-attacks. Adalberto Carrasquilla is the creative hub in midfield, with José Fajardo and Ismael Díaz operating as twin attacking midfielders in support, and Fajardo being the primary goalscorer. Panama's defensive structure has been tight, conceding few goals in qualification. Christiansen and Ghana's former coach Otto Addo were Bundesliga rivals, adding a personal subplot to this encounter.
