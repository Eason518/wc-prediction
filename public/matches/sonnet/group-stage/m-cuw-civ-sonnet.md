## home_note:en

Curaçao make history as the smallest nation by population (≈156,000) ever to play in a FIFA World Cup. Coached by legendary Dutch manager Dick Advocaat, the Blue Wave qualified through CONCACAF with seven wins and three draws. They opened with a historic 7-1 defeat to Germany — but Livano Comenencia scored their first-ever World Cup goal — and face must-win Ecuador in Matchday 2. Entering Matchday 3 without points and needing a miracle, their squad is drawn largely from Dutch lower-league clubs. The occasion itself is the victory. Kick-off 04:00 GMT+8 on June 26 in Philadelphia.
